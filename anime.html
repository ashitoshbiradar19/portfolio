I AM A COMPLETE ANIME FAN

When people casually label anime as “just cartoons,” I respectfully disagree. Anime is far more than that. Unlike typical Western cartoons—which often emphasise slapstick humour, standalone episodes, and simpler animation directed at younger audiences—anime is rooted in Japanese storytelling and artistic tradition. It features intricately detailed designs, expressive visuals, and richly layered themes. Episodes typically run longer, woven into multi-episode arcs that drive character development and complex narratives rather than isolated punch-lines. Anime openly tackles mature, philosophical, and emotional subject matter—ranging from romance and existential dread to societal critique. So no—anime isn’t just a cartoon; it’s a distinct and sophisticated art form with its own cadence, depth and cultural nuance.
